What I found in 30 seconds

The entire site converts to a mailto: link. Every single CTA on the page — from the nav, the hero, and all three package cards — points to an anchor (#contact) that triggers an email to builtbyaaronf@gmail.com. This means a visitor who wants to buy must: click the CTA → hope their email client opens → write an email → wait for a reply → receive a quote → pay an invoice. That is at minimum 5 steps and introduces a 24–72 hour gap before any money changes hands.

The conversion path is not just unoptimised — it does not exist yet as a flow. The biggest single thing you can do for FORGE's revenue is replace the mailto link with a Calendly booking form or a lightweight enquiry form with a 48-hour response guarantee. Every other fix in this report is secondary to that.

CTA Audit — All 7 CTAs
CTA 1: "Get started" — Nav
Click Depth 4+ · HIGH FRICTION
Location Sticky navigation bar (top right)
Type Anchor link styled as button
Destination #contact → mailto: link
"Get started" is generic and tells the visitor nothing about what happens next. It scrolls to the bottom of the page, which then opens their email client. The nav CTA is the highest-visibility real estate on the page — it is in front of the user at all times — and it is wasting that position on a 4-step path to an email inbox.
Optimised Text
"Book a free call"
Optimised Destination
Your Calendly link directly (e.g. calendly.com/yourname/15min) — skip the #contact anchor entirely
Opens the booking flow in one click. Calendly handles the form, the slot selection, and the confirmation. Click depth drops from 4+ to 2.
CTA 2: "Deploy my business" — Hero Primary
Click Depth 4+ · HIGH FRICTION
Location Hero section, above the fold
Type Anchor link → #contact
Destination #contact → mailto: link
The copy uses FORGE's internal language. A small business owner landing on this page has never used the word "deploy" in the context of their marketing. It creates a micro-confusion: what exactly am I deploying? The destination (an email link) is also a massive letdown after bold hero copy — the visitor expects a form or a booking flow, not to be handed a mailto: prompt.
Optimised Text
"Get my free strategy call"
Optimised Destination
Direct Calendly link — or a lightweight enquiry form (Tally.so recommended, free, embeds in one line)
"Get my free strategy call" speaks to the visitor's outcome, not your internal process. Adding "free" lowers commitment. Linking directly to booking removes 3 steps from the path.

Type mailto: link

The conversion endpoint of the entire page is a mailto: link. On many devices — especially mobile — this opens the default mail app (if one is configured). The pre-filled email body asks for "Business name, Business location, Package, Anything else" — which is good content — but forced into the wrong channel. Email is also asynchronous: the visitor sends their enquiry and then waits with no confirmation, no SLA, and no next step communicated.
Optimised Text
"Book my free strategy call" (primary) + "Or send a message →" (secondary, linking to a Tally form)
Optimised Destination
Primary: Calendly 15-min call. Secondary: Tally.so embedded form (free, no-code, collects exactly the fields you need).
Two conversion paths at the bottom captures both visitor types — the one ready to talk now (Calendly) and the one who wants to enquire first (form). Both are faster and more reliable than email.
Form Audit
The Contact "Form" — #contact section

⚠ There is no form on the site. The conversion endpoint is a mailto: link with a pre-filled email body. The fields below are what the email body asks for — but they are not enforced, collected, or stored anywhere.

Required
Business nameNeeded to begin research (SCOUT phase). Keep this as field 1 in any form.
Required
Email addressNot currently asked in the email template — needs to be added. You have no way to follow up if the visitor doesn't include it.
Required
Business locationUsed for local SEO targeting and competitor research. Keep it.
Required
Package choice (Quick / Standard / Full)Currently asked but freeform. Make this a dropdown in a proper form so it can't be skipped or misread.
Defer
Phone numberNot currently asked. Don't add it to the form — it increases abandonment. Collect it on the confirmation page or in the follow-up email.
Defer
"Anything else"Currently in the email template. Optional in a form — makes the form feel longer without adding required value. Move to optional field or remove entirely.
Optimised Form (in order)
1. Business name (text, required)
2. Your email (email, required)
3. Business location / area (text, required)
4. Which package? (dropdown: Quick / Standard / Full, required)
5. Anything else? (textarea, optional — label it "optional")

Fields: 4 required + 1 optional. Estimated completion time: under 60 seconds.
